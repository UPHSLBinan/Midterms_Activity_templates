Global warming refers to the long-term increase in the Earth's average surface temperature due to the accumulation of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. The primary cause of global warming is human activities, such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation, which release large amounts of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. The consequences of global warming are far-reaching and include rising sea levels, more frequent and severe natural disasters, and the extinction of plant and animal species. Urgent action is needed to mitigate the effects of global warming, such as reducing greenhouse gas emissions and transitioning to renewable energy sources. Failure to address global warming could lead to catastrophic consequences for the planet and future generations.
