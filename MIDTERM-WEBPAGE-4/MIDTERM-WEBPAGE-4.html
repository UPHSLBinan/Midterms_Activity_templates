Global warming is a phenomenon that refers to the gradual increase in the Earth's average surface temperature due to the emission of greenhouse gases, primarily carbon dioxide, into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun and prevent it from escaping into space, resulting in a gradual rise in temperature. The effects of global warming are widespread and far-reaching, including rising sea levels, more frequent and severe weather events, and changes in ecosystems and wildlife habitats. Scientists have concluded that human activities, such as burning fossil fuels for energy, deforestation, and industrial processes, are the primary drivers of global warming. While the effects of global warming are already being felt in many parts of the world, there is still hope for mitigation through reducing greenhouse gas emissions and adopting sustainable practices to protect our planet for future generations.
